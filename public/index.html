HOW TO TEST YOUR SOIL

1. TAKE A SAMPLE

Collect soil from different spots in your field. Mix them in a clean bucket to get a fair average of your field's health.

2. ENTER THE VALUES

Enter your Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium (N-P-K), and pH levels from your lab report into our digital assistant.

3. GET YOUR GUIDE

Get instant advice on which fertilizers to use and how much you can expect your crops to grow this season.

THE IMPORTANCE OF SOIL pH

Soil pH dictates nutrient availability. Even if your N-P-K levels are perfectly balanced, an extremely acidic (pH < 5.5) or highly alkaline (pH> 8.0) soil will chemically bind nutrients, making them completely inaccessible to plant roots. Target a neutral pH range (6.0 - 7.5) for optimal fertilizer utilization and maximum yield.
